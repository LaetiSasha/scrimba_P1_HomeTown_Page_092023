Top 3 activities to do in Limoges

Go for a wolk along the river

The Vienne River meanders through Limoges, and there are well-maintained promenades and walking paths along its banks. As you start your walk, you'll likely encounter charming cobblestone paths, lined with trees and benches, making it an ideal place for a leisurely stroll.

Discover the impressive exhibitions of the Bernardaud Foundation

Limoges is world-famous for its porcelain production, and you can immerse yourself in this tradition while walking along the Vienne. The porcelain district, where many workshops and factories are located, can be a fascinating detour from your river stroll. You might even find some porcelain shops to browse for souvenirs.

Stroll trough the quartier de la Boucherie

The Quartier de la Boucherie, also known as the Butcher's Quarter, is a historic district located in Limoges. This neighborhood is known for its medieval charm, narrow winding streets, and its historical association with the butchers' trade. Here's more information about the Quartier de la Boucherie.

Traditionally made with black cherries, the dish consists of fruit (typically cherries) covered in a thick, flan-like batter and baked until golden brown. One fun fact about my hometown

The word "limoger" is believed to have originated from the name of a town called Limoges.
In the 19th century, during the July Monarchy in France, the town of Limoges was associated with political intrigue and reshuffling of military officers. Here's the fun fact: The term "limoger" became associated with the practice of removing or reassigning military officers from their posts. It was often used to describe the act of sending an officer to Limoges as a form of disciplinary action or to remove them from their current position.
Over time, this term evolved to encompass the broader meaning of dismissing or removing someone from any position, not just military. So, when you use the term "limoger" in French to mean "to dismiss" or "to remove from a position," you're actually using a word with historical ties to a specific town and a particular historical context.
